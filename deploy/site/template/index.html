Findings

Three results, all measured

0.49 vs 0.09

The market knows early

Mean "Yes" price of eventual winners vs losers, 120 days before resolution. Prediction markets are well calibrated months ahead — there is very little left for social media to anticipate.

3 platforms

Platforms specialise by domain

Reddit (89% coverage) is balanced across all domains; Bluesky (82%) is dominated by sport; Telegram (31%) is politics and finance, no sport. Which platform is informative depends on the domain — they are complementary, not redundant.

AUC 0.98 vs 0.70

Social features add nothing to price

Predicting outcomes with temporal cross-validation and a 7-day anti-leakage cutoff: price alone reaches AUC 0.98, the best social features 0.70 — and the combination doesn't beat price. The discourse is already priced in.

Pipeline

From two public APIs to a validated dataset

collect
380 resolved contracts, 95k price snapshots (Gamma/CLOB) · 6.5k Reddit + 48k Bluesky posts · 111k Telegram messages
link
keyword recall → MPNet semantic filter → Gemini LLM-as-judge, validated per platform (κ = 0.43–0.50)
enrich
language detection, RoBERTa sentiment on 36k English posts, spaCy NER, per-pair dedup
store
immutable raw JSONL → Parquet queried in-place by DuckDB (measured 100× faster than Spark here)
analyse
lead/lag cross-correlation, sentiment direction tests, outcome prediction with temporal CV

Engineering

Choices I can defend

DuckDB over Spark — measured, not assumed

Same aggregation on both engines, data replicated up to 200×: 0.09s vs 9.94s on the real dataset. Break-even extrapolates to ~10⁸ rows. "Big" is a property of the data, not a default stack.

Linking validated, not asserted

The contract-to-post linking is the scientific core: three WTI contracts share the same keywords. A funnel (keywords → embeddings → LLM judge) turns "it looks right" into κ = 0.434 on a stratified sample.

Anti-leakage by construction

Every prediction feature — social and price — stops 7 days before resolution; validation is walk-forward on chronologically ordered contracts. The honest result (social adds nothing) survives the setup.

A negative result, kept

Five cross-encoders failed to beat the bi-encoder (pre-registered threshold κ > 0.55). The failure is diagnostic — these models learn topical relevance, not claim-level matching — and it is documented in the decision log.
